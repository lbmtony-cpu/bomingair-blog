Air Balance Test on Commercial Package Unit Improves Performance

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Chino Hills, CA

Why an Air Balance Test Matters for Commercial Package Units

Commercial package units often serve large spaces with varying cooling and heating demands. Over time, ductwork, dampers, and blower settings can drift out of balance, leading to uneven temperatures and higher energy use. An air balance test on commercial package unit identifies these issues by measuring actual airflow at each diffuser and comparing it to design specifications.

The Testing Process Step by Step

Technicians begin by reviewing the unit’s original airflow requirements and inspecting accessible duct runs. They then use calibrated instruments to record velocity and static pressure at supply and return points. Dampers are adjusted incrementally while re-measuring until each zone receives the correct volume of conditioned air. Finally, the package unit’s fan speed and economizer settings are fine-tuned to maintain overall system balance without excessive energy draw.

Benefits Observed After the Test

Properly balanced airflow eliminates hot and cold spots, allowing thermostats to satisfy setpoints more consistently. The unit no longer overworks to compensate for under-served areas, which improves reliability and can extend equipment life. Businesses also notice steadier humidity control and quieter operation once excessive air velocity through certain diffusers is corrected.

Long-Term Value for Southern California Properties

Regular air balance testing helps commercial facilities maintain efficiency amid changing occupancy patterns and seasonal loads common in the region. By documenting readings and adjustments, technicians create a baseline that simplifies future maintenance and troubleshooting. Property managers gain clearer insight into system performance without needing full equipment replacement.

Frequently asked questions

How long does an air balance test on a commercial package unit usually take?

Most tests on a single rooftop unit are completed in one business day, depending on the number of zones and accessibility of ductwork.

Will an air balance test reduce my energy bills?

When airflow is corrected, the package unit runs more efficiently and avoids over-conditioning, often resulting in measurable energy savings over time.

How often should commercial properties schedule this service?

Annual testing is recommended, especially after any duct modifications or when temperature complaints become frequent.
