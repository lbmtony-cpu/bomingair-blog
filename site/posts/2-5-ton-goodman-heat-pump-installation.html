2.5 Ton Goodman Heat Pump Installation Case Study

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Montclair, CA

Project Overview

BOMING Air Conditioning & Heating completed installation of a 2.5 ton Goodman heat pump system. The project included a new outdoor condenser placed on a dedicated pad and an indoor air handler with evaporator coil positioned in the utility room beside the existing water heater.

Why a 2.5-Ton Goodman Heat Pump Fits Southern California Homes

A 2.5-ton unit delivers balanced capacity for many single-story and smaller two-story residences common in the region. Goodman heat pumps provide reliable heating and cooling in moderate climates while maintaining straightforward maintenance requirements. The equipment supports efficient year-round operation without oversized capacity that can lead to short cycling.

Installation Process

Technicians positioned the outdoor condenser on a level pad with proper clearances for airflow. Inside, the air handler and coil were integrated with existing ductwork and connected to the new refrigerant lines. Electrical and thermostat wiring were updated to manufacturer specifications. Final steps included system evacuation, charging, and performance testing under both heating and cooling modes.

Homeowner Gains

The homeowner receives a complete heating and cooling solution from a single outdoor unit and matched indoor components. Proper sizing helps maintain consistent temperatures while reducing energy waste. The Goodman equipment carries a strong warranty when registered, and professional installation ensures code compliance and optimal operation.
