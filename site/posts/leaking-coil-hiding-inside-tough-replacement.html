Leaking Coil Hiding Inside: Tough Replacement Job

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Southern California

Identifying the Hidden Problem

During a routine service call at a home in the Southern California area, our technician noticed reduced cooling performance and signs of moisture near the air handler. Further inspection revealed a leaking evaporator coil tucked deep inside the system, hidden behind ductwork and other components. The leak had gone unnoticed until it began affecting indoor comfort and humidity levels.

Navigating a Difficult Replacement

Accessing the coil proved challenging. The unit's placement required partial disassembly of surrounding panels and careful maneuvering to avoid damaging adjacent refrigerant lines or electrical connections. Our team worked methodically to recover refrigerant, remove the old coil, and install a properly sized replacement that matched the existing system's specifications. Every connection was pressure-tested before reassembly to ensure long-term integrity.

Why Proper Coil Replacement Improves Performance

A leaking coil allows refrigerant to escape, forcing the compressor to work harder and reducing overall efficiency. Replacing it restores the system's ability to transfer heat effectively, which helps maintain consistent temperatures and lowers the risk of future breakdowns. Using a matched coil also preserves the manufacturer's warranty and supports steady airflow across the fins.

What Homeowners Gain from the Repair

After completion, the system returned to normal operation with improved humidity control and quieter performance. The homeowner now experiences reliable cooling without the worry of recurring leaks or unexpected shutdowns during peak summer months. Regular maintenance can help catch similar issues earlier, extending equipment life in the Chino Hills area climate.

Frequently asked questions

How do you find a leaking coil that is hidden?

Technicians use pressure checks, visual inspection, and electronic leak detectors to locate coils that are not easily visible.

Is replacing a coil a major job?

It can be labor-intensive when access is limited, but proper technique keeps the rest of the system intact and restores normal function.

Will a new coil improve efficiency?

Yes, a leak-free coil allows the system to operate at its designed capacity, reducing energy waste and improving comfort.
