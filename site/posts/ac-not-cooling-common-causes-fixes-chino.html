Clogged Filters and Dirty Coils: The Dust Problem

Fine dust carried by Santa Ana winds quickly builds up on filters and outdoor coils. A dirty filter restricts airflow, causing the evaporator coil to freeze and warm air to blow from vents. Homeowners can replace standard 1-inch filters every 30 days during peak summer. For pleated or higher-MERV filters, check monthly and swap when visibly gray. Outdoor condenser coils collect the same dust plus pollen; a gentle rinse with a garden hose on a cool morning can restore efficiency if the unit is accessible and you are comfortable doing basic exterior cleaning.

Refrigerant Leaks and Low Levels

Low refrigerant is a leading cause of warm air from vents. Leaks often develop at copper line connections or older coils in homes built before 2000. Adding refrigerant without locating and repairing the leak is only a temporary fix and is illegal under EPA rules. This issue requires a licensed technician with proper recovery equipment. In Ontario and surrounding communities, technicians frequently encounter the same problem in homes of similar age.

Thermostat and Electrical Component Failures

A miscalibrated thermostat or failing capacitor can prevent the compressor from starting even when the thermostat calls for cooling. Homeowners can verify settings are on cool and a few degrees below room temperature, then check that the fan switch is set to auto. If the outdoor unit makes a humming sound but does not start, the run capacitor is a common failure point in our hot climate. Replacing capacitors involves working with live electrical components and should be left to professionals.

DIY Troubleshooting Steps Before Calling a Pro

Begin by confirming the thermostat is functioning and batteries are fresh. Inspect and replace the air filter if it has not been changed in the past month. Clear any visible debris from around the outdoor unit, maintaining at least two feet of clearance. Listen for unusual noises and note whether the indoor fan runs but the outdoor unit stays silent. These steps resolve many simple issues and help you provide useful details when you contact a technician.

Frequently asked questions

How often should I change my AC filter in Chino during summer?

Check monthly and replace every 30 days when running the system daily in hot, dusty conditions.

Can I add refrigerant myself if my AC is low?

No. Handling refrigerant requires EPA certification and leak repair; only licensed technicians can perform this work legally.

Why does my AC blow warm air only in the afternoon?

Afternoon heat combined with dust buildup on coils often reduces capacity; a professional cleaning or component check usually resolves it.

Is it normal for my electric bill to spike in Chino summers?

Some increase is expected, but sudden large jumps often indicate restricted airflow or low refrigerant that needs diagnosis.
