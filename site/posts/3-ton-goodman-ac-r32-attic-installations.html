Features of R32 Refrigerant in Modern Systems

R32 offers improved efficiency and lower environmental impact compared with older refrigerants. Homeowners in Southern California often see it specified for new residential cooling equipment because it carries less global warming potential while maintaining strong performance in warm climates.

Attic Placement Considerations

Placing the air handler or packaged unit in the attic keeps living spaces quiet and frees up yard or closet areas. Proper ductwork and refrigerant line routing, visible in typical attic photos, must account for insulation, access panels, and condensate drainage to prevent moisture issues common in the region.

System Capacity and Equipment Choice

A 3-ton Goodman unit suits many single-story or modestly sized homes. The equipment shown includes the outdoor condensing section paired with attic-mounted components, connected by insulated piping and flexible ducts that distribute conditioned air throughout the house.

Selecting a Licensed Installer

Correct refrigerant handling, electrical connections, and airflow balancing require EPA certification and local permits. Frequently asked questions

Is R32 refrigerant safe for residential use?

Yes, R32 is classified as mildly flammable but meets current safety standards when installed by licensed technicians following manufacturer guidelines.

Why locate the unit in the attic?

Attic placement reduces indoor noise and preserves outdoor space while allowing straightforward duct connections to multiple rooms.

Does every 3-ton Goodman system use R32?

Newer Goodman models increasingly ship with R32; confirm the exact refrigerant type on the equipment nameplate or with your installer.
