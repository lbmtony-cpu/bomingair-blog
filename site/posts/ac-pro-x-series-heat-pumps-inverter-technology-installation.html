AC PRO X Series Heat Pumps with Inverter Technology Installation

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Eastvale, CA

Project Overview

A homeowner in the Southern California area contacted BOMING Air Conditioning & Heating to replace an aging HVAC system. The chosen solution was the installation of AC PRO X series heat pumps with inverter technology. This equipment provides both heating and cooling in one efficient package, suitable for the region's varying temperatures.

Why Choose AC PRO X Series Heat Pumps with Inverter Technology

The AC PRO X series stands out for its inverter-driven compressor, which adjusts speed based on demand rather than cycling on and off. This design reduces energy use and maintains steadier indoor temperatures. For homes in the Chino Hills area, where summers are warm and winters can require supplemental heat, the system offers reliable performance without excessive utility costs. The units are also designed for quieter operation compared to older single-stage models.

Installation Process

Technicians began by removing the existing equipment and preparing the site for the new AC PRO X series units. Proper sizing ensured the heat pumps matched the home's load requirements. Linesets were inspected and updated where needed, and the indoor air handler was positioned for optimal airflow. Electrical connections and refrigerant lines were completed following manufacturer guidelines. Final testing confirmed correct operation in both heating and cooling modes.

Customer Benefits

After the installation of AC PRO X series heat pumps with inverter technology, the homeowners reported more consistent comfort throughout the day. The variable-speed operation helps lower monthly energy expenses while extending equipment life through reduced wear. The system also includes advanced filtration options that support better indoor air quality. Overall, the upgrade delivers dependable heating and cooling tailored to Southern California conditions.

What makes inverter technology different in heat pumps?

Inverter technology allows the compressor to vary its speed, providing more precise temperature control and better energy efficiency than traditional systems.

Is the AC PRO X series suitable for Southern California homes?
