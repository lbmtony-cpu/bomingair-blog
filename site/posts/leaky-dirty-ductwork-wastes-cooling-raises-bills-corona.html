Corona Homes: Leaky Ducts Driving Up Your AC Bills

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Corona, CA

Living in Corona means dealing with long, hot summers where the AC runs almost nonstop. When ducts leak or fill with dust, that cooled air never reaches your rooms, forcing your system to work harder and driving up electric bills fast.

Why Duct Problems Show Up in Corona and Nearby Areas

Southern California’s dry heat, combined with Santa Ana winds, pulls fine dust through vents and into ductwork. Many homes in Corona, along with nearby Riverside and Ontario, were built decades ago with duct systems that have aged, settled, or developed gaps at joints. These issues are common in the region’s older housing stock.

How Leaks and Dirt Waste Cooling

Leaky ducts let conditioned air escape into attics or crawl spaces instead of your living areas. Dirty ducts restrict airflow, so the AC runs longer cycles to reach the thermostat setting. The result is higher energy use and uneven temperatures throughout the house.

Signs Your Ducts Need Attention

  • Rooms that stay warmer than others even when the AC is running
  • Visible dust buildup around vents
  • Higher-than-usual electric bills during peak summer months
  • Musty smells when the system turns on

Simple Checks You Can Do Yourself

Start by feeling for air coming from vents while the AC runs. Walk through the house and note any rooms that feel noticeably warmer. Look at exposed ducts in the attic or garage for obvious gaps or loose tape. Replace or clean your air filter monthly during heavy use seasons—this small step improves airflow immediately.

When to Call a Local HVAC Pro

If basic filter changes don’t improve comfort or bills stay high, it’s time for a professional duct inspection. Technicians use tools to measure airflow and locate hidden leaks that homeowners can’t see. Sealing and cleaning work is best left to pros because it often involves specialized equipment and proper sealing materials rated for HVAC use.

Frequently asked questions

How do I know if my ducts are leaking in Corona?

Uneven room temperatures, high electric bills, and visible dust at vents are common clues. A pro can confirm with airflow testing.

Can I seal my own ducts?

Minor accessible gaps can sometimes be taped, but most leaks are hidden and require proper mastic or aerosol sealing by a technician.

Does dirty ductwork affect AC performance?

Yes. Built-up dust restricts airflow, making the system run longer and use more electricity to cool the same space.

How often should ducts be inspected in SoCal?

Every 3–5 years is reasonable, especially in dusty areas near the desert or after major wind events.
