Transform a Water Heater AC System into a Heat Pump

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Southern California

Project Overview

A homeowner in the Southern California area needed to update an aging setup that combined a water heater with an air conditioning system. The existing equipment struggled to deliver consistent comfort year-round. The decision was made to transform a water heater AC system into a heat pump, creating a single efficient unit for both heating and cooling needs.

Why a Heat Pump Conversion Made Sense

Heat pumps move heat rather than generate it, which suits the mild climate here in the Chino Hills area. This approach reduces energy use compared to separate heating and cooling appliances. It also eliminates the need for a dedicated furnace while maintaining reliable hot water production through the integrated design. The result is simpler maintenance and fewer points of failure over time.

Installation Steps

Technicians first evaluated the current water heater AC system to confirm compatibility. Old components were carefully removed to avoid disrupting the home. The new heat pump was positioned for optimal airflow and connected to existing ductwork and plumbing. Electrical and refrigerant lines were updated to meet current codes. Final testing verified proper operation in both heating and cooling modes before the system was handed over.

Customer Outcomes

The homeowner now enjoys steady indoor temperatures with lower operating costs. The heat pump provides quiet performance and improved humidity control during warmer months. Long-term reliability is enhanced because the system uses proven technology suited to Southern California conditions. Routine filter changes and annual professional checks are the main requirements for continued efficiency.
