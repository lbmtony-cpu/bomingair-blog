2.5 Ton Day & Night Package Unit Installation

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Southern California

Project Overview

BOMING Air Conditioning & Heating recently completed the installation of a 2.5 Ton Day & Night Package unit at a home in the Southern California area. The existing system had reached the end of its reliable service life, prompting the homeowner to seek a straightforward replacement that would restore consistent cooling without major structural changes.

Why Choose a 2.5 Ton Day & Night Package Unit

A package unit combines the condenser, evaporator, and blower into a single outdoor cabinet. This design works well for properties with limited indoor space. The 2.5-ton capacity matches the cooling load of many single-story homes in the region. Day & Night equipment is selected for its durable construction and straightforward maintenance access, which helps keep long-term operating costs predictable.

Installation Process

Technicians began by safely removing the old unit and inspecting the existing curb and duct connections. The new 2.5 Ton Day & Night Package unit was positioned and secured, followed by new electrical whip, condensate drain, and control wiring. All refrigerant lines were pressure-tested, the system was charged according to manufacturer specifications, and final performance checks confirmed proper airflow and temperature differential.

Results and Homeowner Benefits

The completed installation provides reliable cooling through hot summer months and supports lower humidity levels indoors. Homeowners gain a single-point maintenance location and reduced risk of refrigerant leaks associated with split systems. Regular filter changes and annual professional tune-ups will help the unit maintain efficiency over its expected service life.

Frequently asked questions

What is a package unit?

A package unit is an all-in-one HVAC system that houses heating and cooling components in a single outdoor cabinet, often placed on the roof or a concrete pad.

How long does a 2.5 Ton Day & Night Package unit installation take?

Most straightforward replacements are completed in one day, including removal of the old equipment, placement of the new unit, and system testing.

Is a 2.5-ton unit suitable for my home?

Proper sizing depends on square footage, insulation, and local climate. A load calculation performed on site determines the correct capacity.
