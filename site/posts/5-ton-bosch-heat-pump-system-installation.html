5-Ton BOSCH Heat Pump System Installation

By BOMING Air · July 1, 2026 · Serving Southern California

Project Background

A property owner in the Southern California area contacted BOMING Air Conditioning & Heating to replace an aging HVAC unit. The existing system struggled with consistent temperatures and rising energy use. After an on-site assessment, the team recommended the Installation of 5-Ton BOSCH Heat Pump system to match the home's cooling and heating load.

Why the 5-Ton BOSCH Heat Pump

The BOSCH 5-ton model offers variable-speed operation and strong efficiency ratings. It provides reliable heating even on cooler nights common in the region while delivering steady cooling during summer. The equipment's inverter technology helps reduce short cycling and supports quieter performance compared with older single-stage units.

Installation Steps

Technicians first removed the old outdoor condenser and indoor air handler. New line sets were installed where needed and properly insulated. The new BOSCH outdoor unit was leveled on a composite pad, connected to the indoor coil, and integrated with updated controls. Electrical work included verifying circuit capacity and adding a new disconnect. Final steps covered refrigerant charging, airflow testing, and system commissioning.

Results for the Homeowner

The new system now maintains even temperatures across the house with lower operating noise. Homeowners report more comfortable conditions year-round and notice reduced energy consumption during peak seasons. Routine maintenance visits will help preserve these benefits over time.
